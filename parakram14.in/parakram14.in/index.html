In this era of computerization and globalization where our lives have become so stressful, sports and games have not just become a getaway for recreation, but they have also imparted values like discipline, responsibility, self-confidence, sacrifice and accountability. This is exactly what the PARAKRAM aims to leave behind for each and every one in JUIT, Waknaghat.
Sponsored by sports giants like NIVIA, PARAKRAM the open sports festival will be held in one of the most beautiful campuses one could ever imagine-JUIT, situated in the state of mighty Himalayas, Himachal Pradesh. On November 2014, the university will once again open its gates for thousands of participants from esteemed institutes. The major events being, VOLLEYBALL, BASKETBALL, BADMINTON & TABLE TENNIS, the spectators will rise on their toes and unanimously cheer the spirit of sports and athleticism.
We invite you to be a witness of this momentous sports festival and live up to the glory of sports. With zeal in our hearts, we open up a doorstep for future glories to come.

Basket Ball

Both boys and girls team. Each team shall consist of:
1. No more than twelve (12) team members entitled to play, including a captain.
2. A coach and, if a team wishes, an assistant coach.
3. A maximum of three (3) team followers who may sit on the team bench and have special responsibilities, e.g. manager, doctor, physiotherapist, statistician, interpreter, etc.
4. FIBA rules applicable.
5. 10 minutes each quarter with 2 minute break between the quarters and 5 minutes at half time (unless otherwise stated).
6. Teams should report to the basket ball court half an hour before their match. All the players of the team should have same colour of jersey with printed numbers. Use of abusive or foul language during the play can lead to technical fouls or expulsion depending on the referee and the organizing committee.
7. The decision given by the match official will be considered as the final decision.

Table Tennis

1. Team Size: 4 players There will be 3-single games per match.
a. A vs X
b. B vs Y
c. C vs Z
2. The team to win first two matches will be the winner.The winner of a match is the one who wins the first THREE sets of his and the team which wins the first three games is the winner 3. A game consists of 11 points. There must be a gap of at least two points between opponents.
4. If the score is 10-10, the game goes in to extra play until one of the players has gained a lead of 2 points.
5. The point goes to the player who successfully ends a rally.
6. The score of the server is always announced first.
7. Standard ITTF rules will be followed.

Volley Ball

1. Team Size: 12 players (6+6) (For both boys and girls)
2. Rotation will be followed in the game
3. 3 sets per match
4. Teams must have uniform jerseys with numbers on the back.
5. All standard FIVB rules will be followed.
6. In case of any dispute during the match, the decision of the referees will be considered final and binding.
7. Any disciplinary violation by any member of the team will lead to disqualification of the team.



Badminton

1. Team Size: 5 players. (4 minimum) Both girls and boys team.
2. All the matches will be played in the pattern of total 3 matches. A team will have to win at least 2 out of the 3 matches in order to win. The matches will be played in the following order: a. 1st singles
b. 1st doubles
c. 2nd singles
3. Each of the 3 matches will be played as a set of 3 i.e. Each singles and each doubles will be played as a best of 3 match.
4. One player is allowed to play one singles and one doubles only. No player can play more than one singles or more than one doubles.
5. Matches will be of 21 points. Matches will be played with Yonex Nylon Shuttles.
6. A player will not be allowed to play unless he has Non-Marking Shoes. Matches will be played in indoor court.
7. The latest general rules of Badminton World Federation shall be applied.
8. The decision given by the match official will be the final decision.
